This space is dedicated to sharing thoughts, experiences, and reflections. It’s a canvas for your voice, your ideas, and your stories. Whether you’re here to express your personal journey, explore new perspectives, or simply share what’s on your mind, you’re in the right place.

We encourage respectful and constructive dialogue. Engaging with others in a meaningful way can lead to insightful conversations and mutual understanding. When responding to posts, approach others with empathy and openness, even if you have differing opinions.

Wanted to Be Something Else

Kirisuchan Desu!

Many of us, at some point in our lives, have faced the harsh reality of being compared to others. It’s a wound that often cuts deepest when we’re at our lowest. In those moments of vulnerability, a voice inside whispers, “I’m not enough. They are better than me.” We begin to question our worth, asking ourselves, “Who am I to judge, to stand out, to be worthy?”

But here’s the real question: Do we need to be better than anyone else? Some will say no, insisting that we are unique, incomparable. “You are you,” they say, “and that is enough.” Others argue yes, that the pursuit of being better is essential for personal growth, that it gives us a goal to strive towards, a reason to push forward.

But why? Why must we measure ourselves against others? Why can’t we grow on our own terms, in our own way? No human is perfect; we all need guidance, a compass to navigate the uncertain waters of life. But why are we so consumed with the future, with what might be? The future is unpredictable, unknowable. It cannot be crafted or controlled with certainty. So, why do we allow it to dictate our present?

Perhaps the answer lies not in being better than others, but in being better for ourselves—embracing our flaws, our strengths, our journey. The future will come, whether we obsess over it or not. What truly matters is who we choose to be right now.
